Self-driving Car Simulator

This simulator showcases a self-driving car navigating through traffic using a custom-built, three-layer neural network. The car is equipped with sensors that detect nearby vehicles and obstacles. The neural network processes this sensory data to make real-time decisions, allowing the car to steer, accelerate, or brake as needed.

On the right, you can see a visual representation of the neural network. The network is divided into three layers, each processing different aspects of the input data to determine the car's next move. This visualizer helps in understanding how the inputs from the car's sensors influence its decisions at each level of the network.

The simulator also incorporates a genetic algorithm with a variable mutation rate, allowing you to evolve the car's behavior over time.
